Long Division

Overview

The Long Division exercise produces long division problems of varying difficulty. A long division problem lays out the working that is necessary to reach the answer.

Problem Definition

A problem is defined by its divisor and dividend. From these the answer in the form of a quotient and a remainder can be calculated.

The choice of divisor and dividend is given by the difficulty level. The ranges of values are:

DifficultyDivisorDividend
Easiest11->1314->19
Easy14->19101->999
Medium21->39101->999
Hard41->991001->9999
Hardest51->49910001->99999

Note: The Easiest difficulty level introduces the concept of the remainder. The quotient will always be 1.

It is possible to show a table of multiplication equations for the divisor where the multiplier value is from 2 to 9. These hints allow the student to gauge the largest multiple of the divisor that is smaller than the current dividend.

The number of problems is fixed by difficulty level and there is only a risk of duplication at the Easiest level (which has only 18 unique problems).
