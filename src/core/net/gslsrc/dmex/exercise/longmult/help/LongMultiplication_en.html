Long Multiplication

Overview

Long Multiplication produces standard long multiplication exercises with various lengths of multiplicand and multiplier. The problem is presented as a series of lines:

  1. the multiplicand
  2. the multiplier
  3. 2 or more blank intermediate lines of working calculations
  4. the blank answer line

The number of intermediate lines equals the number of digits in the multiplier.

Problem Definition

A long multiplication problem is defined by its multiplicand (top) and its multiplier (bottom) terms. A problem is defined by selecting a level of difficulty which controls the lengths (number of digits) of the multiplicand and multiplier. These lengths are:

DifficultyMultiplicandMultiplier
Easiest 2 2
Easy 2, 3 2
Medium 3 2, 3
Hard 3, 4 2, 3
Hardest 4 3, 4

The length of the answer is limited to 7 digits to prevent overflow of the problem on the generated page so some combinations of multiplicand and multiplier are not allowed.

Number of Problems

All problem definitions produce in excess of a thousand unique problem so there is no danger of exact duplication on a problem sheet.

Answers

Answers can be provided either as a separate problem sheet, with each problem filled in, or on the same sheet in the right-hand margin.
